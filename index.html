The Data Narrator

Nobody listens to your data until you make them.

Why this book

Data does not change decisions until someone carries people across.

Most organisations are not short of dashboards, reports or governance packs. They are short of meaning that reaches into someone's day and makes the next decision easier.

The Data Narrator is built on one promise: it helps the person whose data gets ignored become the person whose data decides. Under the craft sits a simple truth: data is not the point. People are.

The method

The Bridge carries people from a number they do not care about to a decision they will act on.

01

The near bank

Find the real signal: the true problem, the outcome that matters and what the customer is actually experiencing.

02

The span

Build the story and analogy that carry people across without drowning them in the working.

03

The far bank

Name the decision, the people who have to cross and what the truth will cost them. The far bank is political, not logical.

04

The Canvas

A one-page version of the Bridge you can fill in for any insight, meeting, metric or decision.

Written for

The overlooked data professional and the leader drowning in dashboards.

  • Analysts, data owners and governance leads who are tired of being technically right and organisationally ignored.
  • Senior leaders who need fewer decorative dashboards and more decisions they can trust.
  • Transformation teams trying to surface the truth before risk becomes expensive.
  • Anyone who has learned that the failure is rarely the data. It is whether anyone is willing to cross.

About the author

Rose Attridge

Rose is an independent consultant working across transformation governance, delivery control, data strategy, operating model design and executive reporting.

The book is built from hard-won work across advanced engineering, construction, insurance, retail and energy, where the real barrier was often not the quality of the data but whether capable people were ready, willing or invited to act on it.
